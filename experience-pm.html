Project 01

Samolet Plus — Secondary Real Estate Marketplace

samoletplus.ru ↗

A nationwide marketplace for secondary real estate listings submitted by independent agents across Russian cities. The platform aggregated listings from agents, displayed them on a public-facing website, and was deeply integrated with an external CRM as the primary data source. I inherited this project in crisis — deadlines missed, previous PM gone, stakeholders unhappy — and took it through to production.

20–25
cross-functional team members managed
Apr '22 – Apr '23
project timeline

What I did

Joined a distressed project and took full ownership immediately — ran team dailies on day 2, managed client communication within days, held full project context within a week
Diagnosed the root cause of missed deadlines: an external CRM integration left until the end, with inconsistent and unclean data that the website couldn't display properly
Created a comprehensive issue list, presented it to stakeholders and the external CRM team, and drove agreement on responsibility splits — what each side would fix, process, or accept as-is
Established structured collaboration with the external CRM team: embedded them into the project workflow and coordinated their work directly, not at arm's length
Managed a cross-functional team of 20–25 (engineers, designers, BA/SA, QA) and coordinated 10+ adjacent product teams and external vendors
Led the first production release, then transitioned the project into ongoing support and continuous development
Worked within a large PMO structure — actively contributed to shared processes and standards across the program
Key lesson

External integrations must be started early and validated with real data — before the interface is built. The root cause of the missed deadlines was that CRM integration was left until the end. When the data turned out to be inconsistent, it required weeks of tedious coordination to fix. I now treat external dependencies as the highest-risk items in any project plan.

Project 04

A101 — Interactive Commercial Real Estate Map

a101.ru/commercial/map ↗

A fully custom interactive map for browsing and selecting commercial spaces (retail, office, restaurant units) in A101 developments — properties that were still under construction at the time. There was no standard mapping solution. Designers hand-drew every map layer from architectural plans; the backend sliced them into image tiles with unit outlines and infrastructure points overlaid on top.

Built from scratch
no off-the-shelf mapping solution used
~2021
delivery timeline

What I did

Coordinated the full custom map pipeline: designers → tile slicing (backend) → unit outline overlay → interactive frontend with filtering by type, size, price, floor, and delivery date
Designed and maintained an update process to keep the map in sync with shifting construction timelines, unit reconfigurations, and new development phases — serving as the ongoing point of contact between business and engineering for all map changes
Led a full redesign of the map interface, managing the design and development cycle while ensuring continuity of the live product
Ensured premium design and UX quality — the map itself was the sales tool, so the quality bar was genuinely high
Why this stands out

There was no playbook here — the technical approach, data model, and operational update process all had to be invented together. It required close collaboration with designers and engineers from day one, not just coordination after decisions were made. And the ongoing challenge of keeping a visual product in sync with physical construction made this one of the most interesting process design problems I've worked on.
