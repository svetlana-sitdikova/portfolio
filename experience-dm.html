Project 01

Samolet Ecosystem — Multi-Team Program

samolet.ru ↗

Delivery across the full Samolet digital ecosystem: high-load website (5M+ MAU), customer account with online property purchases, and internal B2B tools. My role was to make 53 engineers, designers, and PMs aligned, unblocked, and shipping — while the team and product were both growing rapidly.

What I did

Coordinated all 6 teams within a Large-Scale Scrum (LeSS) framework — quarterly planning, cross-team dependency management, workload balancing, and priority alignment with the product direction
Hired 5 project managers and acted as their direct manager: onboarding, mentoring, 1-to-1s, supporting them in building strong team processes independently
Built release management from scratch: twice-weekly releases for the 5M MAU website, release calendar, clear guidelines, new roles, and expanded staging environments to reduce risk
Overhauled incident management — classification, prioritization framework, Kanban for bug tracking, automated dashboards, and standardized resolution procedures. CRM bugs dropped from ~400 to <30/month, critical resolution time down to 1–2 days
Directly managed a team of 6 focused on technical initiatives — led site performance improvement from ~7s to <3s via CDN, caching, backend optimization, and Vue 2 → Nuxt 3 migration
Streamlined design processes: standardized review criteria, introduced a design lead role, implemented designer-driven test design before engineering handoff

Project 03

Online Real Estate Transaction Flow

A complex multi-step flow enabling users to purchase an apartment entirely online: personal questionnaire, document generation, digital signing, file uploads, and payment — with deep CRM integration. The original plan was 6 months. When the CPO pushed back, I rebuilt the plan and delivered an MVP in 2.5 months.

6mo→2.5mo

What I did

Managed full delivery: scope definition, estimation, planning, team coordination, integration management, testing, release
Renegotiated scope with CPO and product manager under executive pressure — removed non-critical functionality from MVP to make the timeline realistic without cutting quality
Scaled the team with additional engineers and QA to meet the compressed deadline
Designed parallel workstreams with tech leads — mapped dependencies in advance to prevent teams from blocking each other at critical points
Managed the external CRM integration with a third-party team: embedded them into our workflow and coordinated their work directly — not at arm's length
Hired a PM toward project end and executed a smooth handover — full context transfer, no disruption to delivery
