One of Russia’s largest residential real estate developers, with a fully custom digital ecosystem: a high-load public website (5M+ MAU), a customer account platform for online apartment purchases, several property microsite brands, and internal B2B and CMS tools — all deeply integrated with CRM, payment providers, and third-party services.

The main challenge was the flagship product — the apartment sales website — a legacy monolith with deep dependencies and significant technical debt, worked on simultaneously by multiple teams. That made cross-team transparency in releases and planning not just important, but critical.

I joined as Delivery Manager to coordinate 6 distributed development teams and bring structure to a fast-growing, multi-vendor environment. Over 2+ years, I scaled the engineering org from 35 to 53 people, built release and incident management from the ground up, launched a no-code marketing tool, and led a performance initiative that cut page load from 7s to under 3s.

What I did

Coordinated all 6 teams within a Large-Scale Scrum (LeSS) framework — quarterly planning, cross-team dependency management, workload balancing, and priority alignment
Hired 5 project managers and acted as their direct manager: onboarding, mentoring, 1-to-1s, supporting them in building strong team processes
Built release management: twice-weekly releases for the 5M MAU website, release calendar, clear guidelines, new roles, expanded staging environments
Improved incident management — classification, prioritization framework, Kanban for bug tracking, automated dashboards. CRM bugs dropped from ~400 to <30/month
Led site performance improvement from ~7s to <3s via CDN, caching, and Vue 2 → Nuxt 3 migration
Streamlined design processes: standardized review criteria, introduced a design lead role, implemented designer-driven test design before engineering handoff
